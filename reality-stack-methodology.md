# REP Reality Stack Methodology

## Overview

REP Reality Stack is a structured observational framework designed to model real-world events across multiple territorial scales.

The system organizes daily events using the REP (Reconstructible Event Protocol) structure and distributes them across six nested territorial levels:

WORLD  
EUROPE  
ITALY  
PIEMONTE  
TORINO  
BARRIERA DI MILANO  

Each level records events that may originate locally or derive from higher-level systemic pressures.

The purpose is to transform fragmented information into a coherent, traceable and computationally readable representation of reality.

---

# Core Principle

Reality often unfolds through cascading effects.

A macro event at global scale may produce downstream consequences across multiple territorial levels.

Example:

global cyber instability  
↓  
European regulatory and infrastructure pressure  
↓  
national systemic exposure  
↓  
regional operational dependency  
↓  
urban service impact  
↓  
district-level everyday consequences

REP Reality Stack models these cascades explicitly.

---

# Event Structure

Each event follows the REP model:

decision  
cost  
trace  
time  

These fields represent:

decision  
the structural change or condition identified.

cost  
the operational consequence or pressure generated.

trace  
the observable evidence or explanation of the event.

time  
the moment the event is recorded.

Optional fields may include:

derived_from  
impact_vector  
confidence  
notes

# Territorial Levels

## World

Global systemic events.

Examples:

- geopolitical instability
- global cyber threats
- technological shifts
- economic systemic changes

---

## Europe

Events affecting the European continent or EU institutional framework.

Examples:

- EU regulatory pressure
- continental infrastructure incidents
- cross-border cybersecurity issues

---

## Italy

National-level structural developments affecting Italian systems and infrastructure.

Examples:

- national digital service exposure
- regulatory implementation challenges
- sector-wide disruptions

---

## Piemonte

Regional-level economic, infrastructural and operational consequences.

Examples:

- regional transport systems
- SME digital infrastructure
- regional public services

---

## Torino

Urban operational systems and municipal services.

Examples:

- transport services
- city digital platforms
- municipal governance systems

---

## Barriera di Milano

District-level everyday impact.

Examples:

- citizen digital services
- local economic exposure
- service accessibility

---

# Registry Model

Each level produces a **daily registry**.

A registry contains all events recorded for that level during the day.

Example:

world/registry/rep-world-registry-YYYY-MM-DD.json

---

# Checkpoint Model

Each level produces a **daily checkpoint** summarizing the state of that level.

Fields include:

date  
scope  
event_count  
top_events  
dominant_pressures  
derived_from  
generated_at  

Checkpoints provide a concise operational snapshot.

---

# Daily Stack

All levels are linked together through a master file:

daily-stack/rep-daily-reality-stack-YYYY-MM-DD.json

This file represents the complete vertical structure of the day's events.

---

# Observational Rules

REP Reality Stack follows three strict rules.

## Observation Only

Only publicly observable information is recorded.

No private intelligence or speculation is included.

---

## Structural Neutrality

Events are described technically.

No ideological or political framing is introduced.

---

## Derivation Logic

Events may propagate downward.

Lower-level events may reference higher-level events using the field:

derived_from

This creates explicit causal linkage across scales.

---

# Scientific Value

The methodology allows:

- reconstruction of event cascades
- cross-scale analysis
- long-term pattern observation
- computational analysis of societal dynamics

Instead of isolated narratives, the system produces a structured temporal dataset.

---

# Long-Term Vision

If maintained daily, the repository becomes a historical map of how global events propagate through territorial structures.

Over time it allows:

- trend analysis
- systemic risk observation
- resilience modeling

REP Reality Stack therefore functions as a structured observational layer for real-world dynamics.
